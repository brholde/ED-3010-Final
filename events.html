March 2, 2000

Lunch Boxes To Laptops

The governor of Maine, Angus King, announced a program titled, “From Lunchboxes to Laptops” or “Maine Learning Technology Initiative”. This would be the first state-wide initiative in America to provide laptops to each student. This movement would later spread to other states which provided a market for school monitoring software.

(Watters, 2015)

December 21, 2000

Children's Internet Protection Act (CIPA)

The Children's Internet Protection Act (CIPA) was passed by Congress in America. It is designed to keep children off of sites viewed as dangerous or unsuitable. Schools have to meet its requirements in order to receive certain funding and discounts. Schools tend to adopt device monitoring tools in order to meet these requirements for funding. The following is what schools are required to control and monitor.

    • Access by minors to inappropriate matter on the Internet;
    • The safety and security of minors when using electronic mail, chat rooms and other forms of direct electronic communications;
    • Unauthorized access, including so-called “hacking,” and other unlawful activities by minors online;
    • Unauthorized disclosure, use, and dissemination of personal information regarding minors; and
    • Measures restricting minors' access to materials harmful to them.

June 29, 2021

Emergency Connectivity Fund

Emergency Connectivity Fund was a program that provided 7.171 billion dollars for eligible schools and libraries. This program helped to ensure students were provided the necessary devices required for e-learning. Additionally, the funding was used to provide internet access to those who needed it through hotspot devices. This expanded the number of students who received a device during the pandemic and indirectly led to more student monitoring.
